The problem

Your feelings deserve more than a notes app

📓
Paper diaries get lost, ruined, or read
You pour your heart out and then it lives in a notebook that could be thrown away, soaked in rain, or found by the wrong person.
"I lost 3 years of journals in a move. I'll never get those thoughts back."
📱
Notes apps are cold and clinical
Apple Notes, Google Keep — they were built for grocery lists, not breakdowns at 2am. There's no mood tracking, no warmth, no ritual.
"Writing in Notes feels like filing a report about my own feelings."
☁️
Journal apps want your data
Most journaling apps require accounts, sync your private thoughts to servers, show ads, and charge $8/month for the privilege.
"I don't want a corporation reading about my anxiety at 3am."

what to write

Not sure where to start?
These will guide you

Real things people write in their diaries. Swipe through for inspiration — or just start with whichever one feels right.

💜 feelings
Write about exactly how you feel right now — no filter, no editing. Just let it out.
"I'm exhausted in a way sleep doesn't fix. Not sad exactly — just hollow. Like I've been performing okayness for so long I forgot what real feels like."
💛 gratitude
Name three tiny things from today you're grateful for. The smaller, the better.
"Morning light through the window at 7am. My mum sending a random meme. The fact that I remembered to eat lunch today."
🌊 growth
What's something you learned about yourself this week that surprised you?
"I apologise before I speak in meetings. Every time. I hadn't noticed until someone pointed it out. I don't know what to do with that yet."
🌸 memory
Describe a moment from the last 7 days in so much detail you could relive it.
"We sat on the rooftop until 2am talking about everything and nothing. The city was loud below. The air smelled like rain. I keep going back there in my head."
🌱 future
Write a letter to yourself one year from today. What do you want them to know right now?
"Dear future me — I hope you're gentler with yourself than I am. I hope you chose the scary thing. I hope you remember this year not as the hard one, but as the one where you started."
🔥 hard days
Write about something you've been avoiding thinking about. It won't get smaller by ignoring it.
"I keep telling myself I'm fine with how things ended. I'm not. I'm pretending so thoroughly that some days I almost believe it. But late at night I don't."
